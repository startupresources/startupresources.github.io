Startup Resources



There are many types of startup tools and startup resources to use to boost give your business a good start.



Domain Name Registration and Website hosting

Domain name registration is one of the most important startup tools that your business will use. The domain name is your business card and must suit your business. Domain names can be brought online for a low as $.99 on sites like Godaddy.com and 1&1.com. These sites double as web hosting sites as well. It’s a great all-in-one package.



Company Naming Ideas and Legal & Business Registration

When it comes to naming your company, you must be sure to research the perspective name before you go slapping it on all of your promo merchandise. Company names must be registered if they are not already trademarked. It is wise to do so before opening your doors, online or in person. You can register your business online or in person with your state's Department of Revenue Services. Trademarking your business can be done at The United States Patent and Trademark Office. Look online in their database to see if your business name is already taken.



Project Management, Deployment and Outsourcing

Spread the work around by managing your team or hiring on demand assistants. These can be virtual assistants or a traditional employee. Pass along the tedious and time-consuming work that can be safely handled by a third party to your new temp worker.



Surveys of Customers

Don’t be afraid to bother your customers with fun surveys from the likes of Survey Monkey, a great survey building website.



Design, Mockups & Wireframes

Web design has been made easy for the novice with startup tools such as Canva and Designer to design beautiful graphics for your website. For the site, it’s self you can use sites such as Wordpress or Wix. No coding required.



Development

Invest time and energy in product and business development.



E-commerce

Sites like Shopify, eBay and Amazon are great for setting up e-commerce in a snap. Just list and sell.



Payments & Transactions

How do you want to get paid! This may be the most important part. Use reliable resources such as PayPal and Stripe to process secure transactions on your site.



Social Media

Boost your sales and popularity with social media. Use Instagram and Facebook to your best advantage with product tagging and direct sales.



Accounting & Finance Tools

Stay in the track of money with QuickBooks and TurboTax resources. No accountant necessary.



Website analytics

Track how your website is performing with built-in analytics on WordPress. Or add your own with Google Analytics. Startup tools has a large list of available web analytics and other tools for startups.



Bug tracking

Streamline problems that arise with a bug tracking software that follows through.
